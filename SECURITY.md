# Security Policy

At UID.one, we take the security of our decentralized, sovereign identity platform and open-source packages seriously. If you believe you have found a security vulnerability in this repository, we encourage you to report it to us via our responsible disclosure channel.

## Reporting a Vulnerability

**Please do not report security vulnerabilities via public GitHub issues.** Instead, submit your findings to our dedicated security team:

*   **Email:** [security@uid.one](mailto:security@uid.one)

To help us triage and resolve the issue quickly, please include:
1. A descriptive title and type of vulnerability (e.g., DOM-based DLP bypass, session hijacking, origin lockdown escape).
2. Steps to reproduce the issue (including proof-of-concept code, scripts, or screenshots where applicable).
3. The impact of the vulnerability and how it could be exploited.
4. Your name or pseudonym for attribution in release notes (optional).

## Response & Triage Timeline

We pledge to coordinate with you and act transparently:
*   **Acknowledgement & Triage:** Within **24 to 48 hours** of submission.
*   **Severity Assessment & Status Update:** Within **7 days** with an expected timeline for remediation.
*   **Fix Target:** We target resolving High/Critical issues within **14 days** and Medium/Low issues within **30 days**.
*   **Coordinated Disclosure:** We support public disclosure only after a patch has been released and deployed. We ask for a standard **90-day embargo** period from the date of initial report to allow users and downstream dependents to update.

## Bug Bounty Program

To show our appreciation to the security research community, we operate a Bug Bounty Program:
*   **Scope:** Vulnerabilities in this public repository that lead to critical security bypasses, token theft, or cryptographic implementation flaws.
*   **Rewards:**
    *   **Critical Severity:** $200 USD + Attribution on our Security Wall of Fame
    *   **High Severity:** $100 USD + Attribution
    *   **Medium/Low Severity:** Honorable mention/attribution
*   **Rules of Engagement:**
    *   No destructive testing, DoS/DDoS, or social engineering attacks.
    *   Do not exfiltrate or modify user data beyond what is strictly necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability.
    *   Adhere to local laws and GitHub’s Terms of Service.

## Safe Harbor

We consider security research conducted in accordance with this policy to be:
*   Authorized in connection with the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) and equivalent local statutes.
*   Exempt from legal action by UID.one. If legal action is initiated by a third party against you and you have complied with this policy, we will make it known that your actions were authorized and conducted in good faith.

Thank you for helping keep the sovereign identity layer secure for everyone.
